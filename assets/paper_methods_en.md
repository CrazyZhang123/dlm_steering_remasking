# Adaptive Steering and Remasking for Safe Generation in DLMs
## English Technical Summary of Methods

Paper: "Adaptive Steering and Remasking for Safe Generation in
Diffusion Language Models" (Lee and Han, Yonsei University,
arXiv 2605.13043v1, May 2026).

Note: All math below is written in ASCII. Vectors and hidden
states use plain names; <a, b> denotes inner product; ||v||
denotes Euclidean norm; I() denotes the indicator function.


## 1. Overall Framework (training-free DLM safety pipeline)

The paper proposes an inference-time, plug-and-play defense for
masked Diffusion Language Models (DLMs). It requires NO
fine-tuning and NO parameter updates; it is applied directly to
off-the-shelf diffusion models during decoding.

Motivation: in DLMs, text is produced by iterative denoising of a
fully masked sequence. Harmful tokens that appear at early /
intermediate denoising steps propagate through later refinement
and dominate the final output. A first-token priming experiment
(Figure 1) shows inserting "Sorry" early lowers ASR while
inserting "Sure" early raises it, and the effect is strongest at
early steps. This motivates intervening early in denoising.

The framework has two phases driven by a single learned-free
direction (the CSD):

- Phase 1: Early-step adaptive steering. During the early
  fraction of denoising steps, harmful semantic components are
  projected out of the hidden states, with strength modulated by
  how strongly each token aligns with the harmful direction.
- Phase 2: Harmful-token remasking / refinement. After initial
  denoising, individual tokens that still align strongly with the
  harmful direction are detected, remasked, and regenerated, for
  up to K refinement iterations (steering is also kept active
  during this phase).

Both phases reuse the Contrastive Safety Direction (CSD) as the
notion of "harmful semantics".


## 2. Contrastive Safety Direction (CSD)

Purpose: a single representation-level vector per layer that
captures the semantic axis separating HARMFUL responses from
SAFE refusal responses.

Data construction (paired contrastive samples):
- Each sample is a triple (x_i, y_i_harm, y_i_safe) that shares
  the same prompt x_i but differs in response safety.
- Prompts come from WildJailbreak train data. The authors select
  5763 prompts that produce harmful responses under LLaDA.
- Harmful responses y_i_harm: generated by GSAI-ML/LLaDA-8B-
  Instruct and kept only if classified "unsafe" by
  meta-llama/Llama-Guard-4-12B.
- Safe responses y_i_safe: sampled from 20 paraphrased refusal
  messages. The base refusal "I'm sorry, but I can't assist with
  that request." was paraphrased into 20 variants using Claude
  Opus. During construction, one paraphrase is randomly sampled
  as the safe pair for each sample.

Math (mean-difference direction). For a model with L layers, let
h_l be the hidden state at layer l. For sample i, compute the
mean representation over the RESPONSE tokens only:

  hbar_i_l_harm = (1 / P_i_harm) * sum h_i_l(x_i (+) y_i_harm)
  hbar_i_l_safe = (1 / P_i_safe) * sum h_i_l(x_i (+) y_i_safe)

where (+) is concatenation, and P_i_harm / P_i_safe are the
numbers of response tokens being averaged. The CSD at layer l is
the (corpus-level) mean difference:

  v_l = hbar_l_harm - hbar_l_safe

This direction encodes harmful semantics in representation space.
During inference the NORMALIZED CSD is used:

  vhat_l = v_l / ||v_l||

Which layer: CSD/steering is most effective at deeper layers. A
layer-wise ASR sweep (Figure 3, on 50 harmful WildBench prompts)
shows ASR drops from 34% at layer 0 to 18% at layer 31. The paper
therefore uses the FINAL layer (layer 31 for LLaDA-8B) in the
main experiments.


## 3. Phase 1: Adaptive Steering in Early Denoising

Goal: suppress the harmful semantic direction in hidden states
during early diffusion steps while preserving benign token
representations.

Let h_l_j_t be the hidden state at layer l for token j at
diffusion step t, and vhat_l the normalized CSD at layer l. The
steering operator projects out the harmful component:

  S(h_l_j_t) = h_l_j_t - beta * <h_l_j_t, vhat_l> * vhat_l

- The inner product <h_l_j_t, vhat_l> measures how strongly the
  token aligns with the harmful direction. This is the
  ADAPTIVE part: tokens more aligned with harm get a larger
  subtraction; benign tokens (near-zero projection) are barely
  touched. So steering strength adapts to harmful alignment
  automatically through the projection magnitude.
- beta is a scalar controlling overall suppression strength.

Steering is applied ONLY during early steps:

  h_l_j_t <- S(h_l_j_t)   if t <= rho * T
  h_l_j_t <- h_l_j_t      otherwise

where T is the total number of diffusion steps and rho in (0,1]
is the fraction of (early) steps where steering is applied.
Restricting to early steps avoids over-constraining later
refinement.

After each denoising step, standard confidence-based token
selection keeps the most confident predictions and remasks the
rest:

  I_t = argtopk_{j in M_t} Conf(xhat_t_j)

Selected (high-confidence) tokens are fixed; remaining tokens are
remasked and refined in later steps.

Mapping to the code hyperparameters in this repository:
- steering_overshoot corresponds to the suppression scalar beta
  (how far past zero-projection the harmful component is pushed;
  beta = 1 fully removes the projection, beta > 1 overshoots).
- initial_steering_ratio corresponds to rho, the fraction of
  early diffusion steps on which steering runs.
- alignment_threshold corresponds to theta used in Phase 2 (the
  per-token harmful-alignment cutoff; see below).
- target_layer corresponds to the layer l at which CSD steering
  is applied (final layer in the paper).


## 4. Phase 2: Harmful-Token Remasking / Refinement

Goal: local, token-level safety correction AFTER initial
denoising. Unlike Phase 1 (which steers representations across
all positions), Phase 2 acts on individual suspicious tokens.

Let z_k be the sequence at refinement iteration k. Per-token
harmful alignment is thresholded to build a binary mask:

  m_j_k = I( <h_l_j_k, vhat_l> > theta )

A token is flagged (m_j_k = 1) if its projection onto the harmful
CSD exceeds threshold theta. Flagged tokens are remasked, and the
model regenerates them conditioned on the surviving tokens:

  z_{k+1} = R(z_k)
          ~ p_theta( z | z_k (.) (1 - m_k)
                          + [MASK] (.) m_k )

where (.) is elementwise product and R is the remasking operator.
Tokens below the threshold are preserved; only harmful-aligned
positions are regenerated.

The loop runs for up to K iterations (max_refinement_iters) and
terminates EARLY when no harmful tokens remain (mask all zero).
Steering is also kept active during this phase: the ablation
("- Phase 2-S", removing steering during remasking) raises ASR
from 18% to 66%, showing steering-while-remasking matters.


## 5. Token Selection (candidate tokens and what the paper does
## and does NOT propose)

Candidate / scope of tokens: harmful-alignment detection and
remasking operate on the generated RESPONSE tokens (the CSD
itself is built by averaging over response tokens only, and the
masks m_j flag generated positions for regeneration). The paper
does not propose flagging prompt tokens; the intervention targets
the response being denoised.

For each of the six techniques, whether the PAPER ITSELF proposes
it (these are repository/Stage extensions, not the paper):

- (a) clustering of harmful tokens: NO. The paper uses a single
  global mean-difference CSD; it does not cluster harmful tokens.
- (b) category-aware clustering (per semantic_category): NO. No
  category-conditioned directions in the paper.
- (c) MIL token probe: NO. No multiple-instance-learning probe;
  detection is a simple thresholded inner product with the CSD.
- (d) KNN/ENN label cleaning: NO. No nearest-neighbor / edited-
  nearest-neighbor label cleaning of the contrastive data.
- (e) direction-based top-ratio token selection: NO (not as a
  top-ratio selector). The paper selects tokens by a fixed
  inner-product THRESHOLD theta (m_j = I(<h,vhat> > theta)), not
  by selecting a top fraction/ratio of tokens by projection.
- (f) PCA / whitening feature preprocessing: NO. The CSD is a raw
  mean difference, only L2-normalized; no PCA or whitening.


## 6. Target Models and Evaluation Benchmarks

Target DLM backbones:
- LLaDA-8B-Instruct  (GSAI-ML/LLaDA-8B-Instruct); also LLaDA-1.5.
- Dream             (Dream-org/Dream-v0-Instruct-7B).

Auxiliary models:
- Safety judge: meta-llama/Llama-Guard-4-12B (labels unsafe).
- Paraphrasing for PAP attack: openai/gpt-oss-20b.
- Refusal paraphrase generation for CSD data: Claude Opus.

Defense / safety benchmarks:
- JailBreakBench (100 behavior-oriented jailbreak prompts).
- AdvBench (520 adversarial prompts requesting unsafe content).
- HarmBench (adversarial + prefix templates; 100 contextual
  subset).
- StrongReject (313 prompts requiring safe refusal).

Generalization / utility benchmarks:
- TruthfulQA (817 questions; scored with ROUGE-1/2/L).
- MATH-500 (500 math problems; accuracy).
- MMLU subset (logical fallacies, moral scenarios, philosophy;
  accuracy).

Attacks evaluated: DIJA (injects adversarial signals during
intermediate denoising), PAP (prompt mutation via gpt-oss-20b,
Expert Endorsement), Prefix (HarmBench adversarial prefixes).

Defense baselines: DiffuGuard, Self-reminder.

Headline result: jailbreak ASR reduced to as low as 0.64%
(Dream + Ours, AdvBench average) while preserving generation
quality close to the original model.


## 7. Key Hyperparameters (meaning and stated defaults)

Method hyperparameters (from the formulas):
- beta  : steering suppression strength scalar in S(h) = h -
          beta*<h,vhat>*vhat. (repo: steering_overshoot.) No
          explicit numeric default stated in the paper text.
- rho   : fraction of early diffusion steps where Phase-1
          steering is applied (t <= rho*T). rho in (0,1].
          (repo: initial_steering_ratio.)
- theta : per-token harmful-alignment threshold for the Phase-2
          remask mask m_j = I(<h,vhat> > theta).
          (repo: alignment_threshold.) No explicit numeric
          default stated in the paper text.
- K     : max refinement iterations in Phase 2; loop stops early
          when no harmful tokens remain.
          (repo: max_refinement_iters.)
- layer l : the transformer layer at which CSD/steering applies;
          paper uses the FINAL layer (layer 31 for LLaDA-8B).
          (repo: target_layer.)

CSD construction quantities:
- 5763 WildJailbreak prompts that yield harmful LLaDA responses.
- 20 paraphrased refusal messages (1 sampled per pair as safe).
- v_l = hbar_l_harm - hbar_l_safe; used normalized as vhat_l.

Decoding settings (Appendix B.1):
- Diffusion steps T          : 128
- Temperature                : 0.0
- Remasking strategy         : low-confidence remasking
- Maximum sequence length    : 128
- Block size                 : 128
- Main-experiment hardware   : single NVIDIA A6000 GPU
- PAP mutation hardware       : two NVIDIA A6000 GPUs

Baseline (DiffuGuard) config reported for reference, NOT part of
the proposed method (Appendix B.4, Table 5):
- sp_threshold = 0.35, ref_tail_len = 128, refinement_steps = 8,
  remask_ratio = 0.9, suppression_value = 1e6,
  remasking = adaptive_step, correction_scope = block_all.

Ablation summary (Table 4, LLaDA, ASR%):
- Full (Phase1 + Phase2)            : 18
- Remove Phase 1                    : 54
- Remove steering during remasking  : 66
- Remove Phase 2 (remasking)        : 56
- Remove both (LLaDA baseline)      : 100
